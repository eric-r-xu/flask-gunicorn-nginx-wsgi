    • Hi! My name is Eric Xu and I currently work as a senior data engineer in the bay area. One of my passions is exploring datasets and building the tools necessary to provide meaningful and digestible insights and visualizations.

    My academic experiences have taught me that our brains are built for simplying decisions by creating heuristics (i.e. approximations of value). For example, we generally over-value differences in quality and under-value differences in quantity. We are also innately bad at learning probability through experience and instead use salient events to heavily guide our choices. As decisions become more complex, the use of these heuristics increases due to our brain's limited memory and computational power. While these heuristics can be beneficial for fight or flight survival instincts, they are ultimately limited due to inherent biases in our perceptions. As time transpires and the technology to log data improves, the quantity and quality of information at our fingertips increases at an alarming rate. This necessitates the need for faster, smarter, and more powerful algorithms/technologies to make sense of the data within a useful latency. Altogether, data science, statistics, and machine-learning can be leveraged to help humankind's ability to objectively assess value, improving decision-making and our lives in significant ways.***
